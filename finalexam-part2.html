Lorenz Attractor

The Lorenz attractor is an attractor that arises in a simplified system of equations describing the two-dimensional flow of fluid. In the early 1960s, Lorenz accidentally discovered the chaotic behavior of this system when he found that, for a simplified system, periodic solutions of the form

$$\psi = \psi_0 \sin\left(\frac{\pi ax}{H}\right)\sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{H}\right) $$

$$\theta = \theta_0 \cos\left(\frac{\pi ax}{H}\right)\sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{H}\right) $$

grew for Rayleigh numbers larger than the critical value, $Ra>Ra_c$. Furthermore, vastly different results were obtained for very small changes in the initial values, representing one of the earliest discoveries of the so-called butterfly effect.

Lorenz obtained the simplified equations

$$\dot{X} = \sigma(Y-X) $$

$$\dot{Y} = X(\rho-Z)-Y $$

$$\dot{Z} = XY-\beta Z $$

now known as the Lorenz equations.
